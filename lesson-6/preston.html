Try A South African BBQ

In South Africa, on a Friday, Saturday and Sunday tradition across all cultures and age groups in the diverse country is for friends, family and neighbors to have a BBQ. Traditionally anything from chicken, chops, steak and their famous boerawors (a kind of sausage) is cooked on a wood, charcoal or gas fire. This is what is know as a Braai. Friends and family get together, talk, share a meal. A common side with a Braai is a dish made from corn meal called Pap, which is usually topped with a tomato and onion gravy locally known as "Chakalaka".

A Braai is not specific to one group or race or culture, but is truly cross cultural, with each culture having something from anothers at a Braai. Its a time for family, a time for friends, a time to relax and most importantly a time to eat good food. This weekend, why don't you invite soke family and friends over and have yourselves a "Lekker Braai".
